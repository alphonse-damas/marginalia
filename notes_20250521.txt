Marginalia Analytics is evolving into a governance-first analytical evidence evaluation platform designed to assess the trustworthiness, sufficiency, and interpretability of statistical and machine learning outputs.

The project is no longer merely focused on parsing analytical outputs. The deeper objective is now:

AI governance under varying evidence structures

The system is designed to:

evaluate model trustworthiness,
identify weak or missing evidence,
apply governance reasoning,
and expose uncertainty explicitly rather than masking it.

The current phase has produced a major conceptual transition:

Marginalia should evaluate evidence,
not formatting.

This realization fundamentally changed the architectural direction of the platform.

4. Key Insight

One of the most important conceptual breakthroughs from this phase:

The challenge is not information extraction.
The challenge is structure certainty.

Raw outputs may contain the same information as structured forms, but:

machines require deterministic structure,
humans naturally infer meaning from ambiguous layouts,
AI systems must reconstruct structure probabilistically.

This insight fundamentally reframed the project.

The problem is not:

whether the numbers exist.

The problem is:

whether the system can reliably know what each number means.
